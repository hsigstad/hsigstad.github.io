STF & STJ

Health cases from Brazil's Supreme Court and Superior Court of Justice.

Overview

The STF (Supremo Tribunal Federal) is Brazil's constitutional court and final arbiter on constitutional questions. The STJ (Superior Tribunal de Justiça) is the top court for non-constitutional federal law interpretation. Together, they set binding precedents (temas) that lower courts must follow.

Key health-rights themes established by these courts include:

  • STF Tema 793 — joint and several liability of all government levels (Union, states, municipalities) for health provision
  • STJ Tema 106 — obligation to provide off-list medications under certain conditions
  • STJ Temas 84/98 — enforcement mechanisms including fund seizure (bloqueio de verbas) and daily fines (multa diária)

The dataset contains full decision texts from both courts, with columns: tribunal, text, classe, number, date.
