TJSP: Private Plans vs SUS Cases - How Arguments Differ

Overview

The Tribunal de Justiça de São Paulo (TJSP) handles the largest volume of health litigation in Brazil. This memo maps the distinct argumentative structures used in private health plan cases versus SUS (public health) cases.

1. Volume and Distribution

TJSP Health Case Profile (2024 estimates)

Type Annual Volume % of Total
Planos de Saúde (private) ~180,000 55%
SUS (public) ~120,000 37%
Mixed/Other ~25,000 8%

Jurisdictional Organization

  • Varas da Fazenda Pública: SUS cases against State/Municipality
  • Varas Cíveis: Private plan cases
  • Juizados Especiais: Lower-value claims (both types)

2. Fundamental Legal Frameworks

Private Plans

Source Application
CDC (Lei 8.078/90) Consumer protection, abusive clauses
Lei 9.656/98 Health plans regulation
ANS Resolutions Coverage requirements, rol
Contract Specific terms (subject to CDC limits)

SUS Cases

Source Application
CF/88 Art. 196 Constitutional right
Lei 8.080/90 SUS organic law
RENAME/PCDT Incorporated treatments
STF Temas 6, 793, 1.234 requirements

3. Core Argumentative Differences

3.1 The Plaintiff's Burden

Private Plans:

Plaintiff must show:
1. Valid contract exists
2. Treatment was prescribed
3. Plan denied coverage
4. Denial lacks contractual/legal basis

Burden then SHIFTS to plan to justify denial (CDC)

SUS Cases:

Plaintiff must show (post-Tema 6):
1. Medical necessity (prescription)
2. Financial incapacity (if non-incorporated drug)
3. No therapeutic substitute in SUS
4. If not incorporated: CONITEC didn't expressly reject
5. Evidence-based efficacy

Burden remains largely on plaintiff

3.2 The "Medical Prescription" Argument

Private Plans:

Medical prescription creates strong presumption of medical necessity. Plan must prove treatment is experimental or excluded.

SUS Cases:

Medical prescription is necessary but NOT sufficient. Must also show drug meets incorporation criteria or Tema 6 requirements.

3.3 Financial Arguments

Private Plans:

Irrelevant. Plan cannot deny based on cost if treatment is covered. "Reserva do possível" doesn't apply to private contracts.

SUS Cases:

Central issue. "Reserva do possível" (budgetary limits) is a valid defense. But "mínimo existencial" (core rights) trumps budget when life/health at immediate risk.

4. Defendant's Typical Defenses

Private Plan Defenses

Defense Court Response
"Not on ANS rol" Rejected if no effective substitute (Lei 14.454/2022)
"Experimental treatment" Must prove; ANVISA registration defeats this
"Contractual exclusion" Often deemed abusive under CDC
"Waiting period" Doesn't apply to emergencies
"Pre-existing condition" Must have required prior exams (Súmula 609)

State/Municipality Defenses (SUS)

Defense Court Response
"Not incorporated" Valid, but Tema 6 may override
"Wrong federative entity" Tema 793 - solidarity, but can redirect
"Reserva do possível" Valid for non-essential; rejected for life-threatening
"Administrative path not exhausted" Post-SV 60, increasingly accepted
"No evidence of efficacy" Valid - plaintiff must prove
"Therapeutic substitute exists" Valid if substitute is truly equivalent

5. Tutela de Urgência Patterns

Private Plans (Almost Always Granted)

Standard reasoning:

"Presente a probabilidade do direito, consubstanciada na prescrição médica e na existência de contrato válido, bem como o perigo de dano à saúde do autor, defere-se a tutela de urgência."

Denial is rare:

  • Clearly cosmetic procedure
  • Manifestly experimental (no ANVISA)
  • Evident fraud

SUS Cases (More Scrutinized)

Post-2024 reasoning:

"Necessário verificar o cumprimento dos requisitos fixados no Tema 6/STF e a consulta ao NAT-Jus antes da concessão liminar."

Standard questions:

  1. Is drug incorporated (RENAME/PCDT)?
    • Yes → Grant easily
    • No → Apply Tema 6 requirements
  2. Is there therapeutic substitute?
  3. Was administrative request made?
  4. What does NAT-Jus say?

6. Damage Awards Comparison

Private Plans

Situation Typical Moral Damages
Simple denial, quick resolution R$ 5.000 - R$ 10.000
Denial causing treatment delay R$ 10.000 - R$ 20.000
Denial during ongoing treatment R$ 20.000 - R$ 40.000
Denial leading to worsening/death R$ 50.000 - R$ 200.000+

Material damages: Full treatment cost + consequential losses

SUS Cases

Situation Typical Outcome
Medication granted No moral damages (mere exercise of right)
Proven bad faith delay R$ 5.000 - R$ 15.000 (rare)
Treatment denial causing death Possible, but rare and contested

Key difference: Moral damages against the State are harder to obtain; system failure is harder to prove than contractual breach.

7. Recurring Argumentative Patterns

Private Plan Cases - Plaintiff's Winning Arguments

  1. "Rol exemplificativo"

    O rol da ANS é exemplificativo, não podendo o plano negar cobertura quando não há substituto terapêutico eficaz.

  2. "Médico assistente decide"

    Cabe ao médico assistente, e não ao plano de saúde, definir o tratamento adequado.

  3. "Cláusula abusiva"

    A cláusula contratual que exclui cobertura para tratamento prescrito é abusiva à luz do CDC.

  4. "Expectativa legítima"

    O consumidor contrata plano de saúde com legítima expectativa de cobertura ampla.

SUS Cases - Plaintiff's Winning Arguments

  1. "Direito fundamental"

    O direito à saúde é direito fundamental, de eficácia imediata, oponível ao Estado.

  2. "Mínimo existencial"

    O fornecimento do medicamento integra o mínimo existencial, não podendo ser negado por razões orçamentárias.

  3. "Risco de vida"

    Demonstrado o risco de morte ou grave dano à saúde, impõe-se a concessão.

  4. "Tema 6 cumprido"

    O autor comprovou todos os requisitos do Tema 6/STF: hipossuficiência, ausência de substituto, eficácia comprovada.

Defendant's Winning Arguments

Plans:

  1. "Tratamento experimental sem registro ANVISA"
  2. "Procedimento estético sem indicação médica corroborada"
  3. "Fraude comprovada na contratação"

State/SUS:

  1. "Existe substituto terapêutico incorporado ao SUS"
  2. "CONITEC avaliou e não recomendou incorporação"
  3. "Ausência de prova de eficácia baseada em evidências"
  4. "Autor não demonstrou hipossuficiência financeira"

8. TJSP Specialized Structures

CADIP (Centro de Apoio ao Direito Público)

  • Issues thematic guidance on health litigation
  • Publishes the "Caderno de Judicialização da Saúde"
  • Provides judges with technical support

NAT-Jus SP

  • Technical opinions for judges
  • Connected to e-NatJus national system
  • Increasing use post-SV 60

Câmaras Especializadas

  • Câmaras de Direito Público: SUS cases
  • Câmaras de Direito Privado: Plan cases

9. Trends and Evolution

Private Plans

  • Increasing: Mental health coverage disputes
  • Increasing: Home care / home infusion
  • Stable: Oncology treatment denials
  • Decreasing: Rol disputes (after Lei 14.454)

SUS

  • Increasing: Ultra-high-cost drugs (gene therapy)
  • Increasing: Cannabis-based medications
  • Stable: Oncology (APAC system issues)
  • Decreasing: Basic incorporated drugs (administrative improvements)

10. Practical Checklist for Case Analysis

Private Plan Case

  • Contract exists and is valid?
  • Treatment prescribed by licensed physician?
  • ANVISA registered?
  • On ANS rol or no effective substitute?
  • Exclusion clause applies? Is it abusive?
  • Emergency situation?
  • Moral damages elements present?

SUS Case

  • Treatment incorporated (RENAME/PCDT)?
    • Yes → Straightforward grant
    • No → Continue checklist
  • Tema 6 requirements met?
    • Financial incapacity proven?
    • CONITEC didn't reject?
    • No therapeutic substitute?
    • Evidence of efficacy?
  • Correct defendant (Tema 793)?
  • NAT-Jus consulted?
  • Administrative request made (SV 60)?
