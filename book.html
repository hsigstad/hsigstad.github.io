Exam Questions

▶ Question 1: Efficient Allocation of Assembly Costs

A manufacturer sells industrial equipment to buyers. The equipment requires assembly before use. The manufacturer can assemble the equipment at its factory at a cost of €5,000, while buyers can assemble it on-site at a cost of €7,000. However, pre-assembled equipment is bulkier and more fragile, increasing shipping costs by €3,500 compared to unassembled equipment.

Should the contract include a clause requiring the manufacturer to deliver pre-assembled equipment, or should equipment be delivered unassembled with the buyer responsible for assembly?

▶ How to answer
  1. Identify the two possible allocations of the assembly task
  2. Calculate total real resource costs under each allocation (assembly cost + relevant shipping costs)
  3. Compare total costs to determine which allocation minimizes real resource costs
  4. Apply the efficient allocation principle: assign the task to minimize total costs
  5. Note that contract price adjustments are transfers and don't affect the efficiency analysis
▶ Example solution

Step 1: Identify allocations

Option A: Manufacturer assembles (pre-assembled delivery) Option B: Buyer assembles (unassembled delivery)

Step 2: Calculate total real costs

Option A (manufacturer assembles):

  • Assembly cost: €5,000
  • Shipping cost (assembled): baseline + €3,500

Option B (buyer assembles):

  • Assembly cost: €7,000
  • Shipping cost (unassembled): baseline

Step 3: Compare incremental costs

The relevant comparison is the incremental cost difference:

Manufacturer assembly adds: €5,000 (assembly) + €3,500 (extra shipping) = €8,500 Buyer assembly adds: €7,000 (assembly) + €0 (no extra shipping) = €7,000

Incremental cost savings from buyer assembly: €8,500 - €7,000 = €1,500

Step 4: Apply efficient allocation principle

Total real costs are minimized when the buyer assembles the equipment. Even though the manufacturer has lower assembly costs (€5,000 vs €7,000), the additional shipping costs for pre-assembled equipment (€3,500) make manufacturer assembly more expensive overall.

Conclusion: The contract should deliver equipment unassembled with the buyer responsible for assembly. This saves €1,500 in total real resource costs.

Key insight: Efficient task allocation considers all real resource costs affected by the allocation, not just the direct cost of performing the task.
