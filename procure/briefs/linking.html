Linking Court Cases to Procurement Data

Linking Court Cases to Procurement Data

Brief: cohesive narrative for human consumption. Structured content also in docs/methods.md, docs/thinking.md, and docs/data.md.

Overview

We link TJSP court cases (improbidade administrativa) to TCE-SP municipal procurement contracts by extracting procurement process references from court decision text and joining to the ajustes (contracts) dataset.

Court case text  →  regex extraction  →  (municipality, proc_num, proc_year)
                                                    ↓
Ajustes data     →  join on (Município, Número do processo licitatório, Ano)
                                                    ↓
Crosswalk        →  court case × contract line items

Data inputs

Dataset Rows Source
TJSP sentenças (improbidade) 12,433 audit/build/clean/sentenca__TJSP.csv filtered on assunto/classe
TCE-SP ajustes (contracts) 3,991,407 tce_sp/ajustes/ajustes_all.csv (2016–2022)

Extraction pipeline

Script: source/linking/match_court_to_procurement.py

Step 1: Extract procurement references from court text

Three regex patterns, applied in order:

  1. Modality + number (high confidence): matches "pregão nº 15/2012", "convite 26/2010", "Tomadas de Preços números 02/2012", etc. Captures the modality keyword followed by an optional number prefix and a num/year pair. Handles plural forms (convites, cartas-convites, tomadas, concorrências, etc.).

  2. nº X/YYYY with procurement context (medium confidence): matches "nº 26/2010" when a procurement keyword (licitação, pregão, edital, certame, etc.) appears within 300 characters. This catches references where the modality is mentioned separately from the number.

  3. PA prefix (administrative process): matches "PA 3895/2010" with procurement context nearby.

List continuation: After a modality+number match, the parser looks for comma-separated continuations like "02/2012, 03/2012 e 04/2012".

Exclusions:

  • Legal references are detected and excluded (Lei 8.666/1993, Decreto nº X/YYYY, artigo, resolução, etc.)
  • A blocklist of known law numbers (8429/1992, 8666/1993, 14230/2021, 10520/2002, etc.)
  • 2-digit years resolved as: ≤30 → 2000s, >30 → 1900s
  • Years outside 1990–2025 discarded

Step 2: Normalize municipality names

Court cases use the comarca (court jurisdiction) as the municipality identifier. Normalization:

  • Strip accents, lowercase
  • Remove "/ SP" suffix
  • Replace hyphens with spaces
  • Collapse whitespace

307 of 312 comarcas in the data resolve to ajustes municipalities (98.4%).

Step 3: Join to ajustes

Join on (municipality, proc_num, proc_year). The ajustes Número do processo licitatório field has mixed formats: pure numbers (1.0), slash strings ("07/2017"), and alphanumeric codes ("G00002"). We parse the numeric part for matching.

One procurement process typically generates multiple contract line items (different firms, items, or contract amendments), which is why 223 matched references expand to 1,304 crosswalk rows.

Why the match rate is low

The primary bottleneck is time period mismatch. Court cases decided in recent years reference procurement from roughly a decade earlier, because improbidade cases take many years to reach sentença. The ajustes data covers 2016–2022, while most court-referenced procurement dates to 2005–2015.

Match rate by procurement year:

≤2015 ~5,500 0 0%

2021+ ~30 ~25 ~83%

Within the TCE-SP time window (2017+), the match rate is 50–88%. The mechanical linking works well. The gap is data coverage, not methodology.

Extraction quality (regex evaluation)

We evaluated regex extraction against GPT-4o-mini on two independent random samples of 50 cases each.

Evaluation methodology

  1. Sample 50 cases randomly, extract a text window around procurement keywords
  2. Run GPT-4o-mini with a structured prompt asking for all procurement process references
  3. Compare LLM output to regex output at the reference level

Results

After careful analysis of every false negative, we found:

  • Regex recall: ~96–100% against true procurement references
  • Most apparent "false negatives" were actually LLM errors: hallucinated numbers from year mentions, non-procurement references (concurso público, decreto, inquérito civil, TC process numbers)
  • Only 4 real misses across both samples, all from list continuation items with plural modality forms — fixed by adding plural support

The regex has high recall for procurement references. Precision is less critical because false positive extractions simply fail to match in the ajustes join and drop out.

Potential improvements

1. Obtain older TCE-SP data (highest impact)

The single most impactful improvement would be obtaining ajustes data for pre-2016 procurement. This would cover the ~5,500 references currently unmatched due to time period. TCE-SP's Audesp system likely has earlier data — the Portal da Transparência Municipal may provide it on request.

Expected gain: Could potentially match 2,000+ additional references if pre-2010 data is available.

2. CNPJ-based linking (complementary)

~650 court cases mention firm CNPJs in the text. These can be matched directly to CNPJ da empresa contratada in ajustes without needing a procurement process number. This is a complementary strategy that works even for cases describing contratação sem licitação (contracting without competitive bidding), where no formal process number exists.

3. Firm-name fuzzy matching

Most court cases name the firms involved. Fuzzy matching firm names from court text to Nome da empresa contratada in ajustes (within the same municipality) could link cases where the process number is not mentioned or where the procurement was informal.

4. Expanding the court case universe

The current 12,433 improbidade cases are filtered from 65,199 TJSP sentenças. More recent cases (2023–2025) would reference procurement in the 2017+ window where match rates are high. Obtaining an updated sentença dataset would directly increase matches.

5. LLM extraction (marginal improvement)

Full LLM-based extraction was evaluated and found to provide minimal improvement over the context-aware regex. The regex already captures ~96–100% of true procurement references. LLM extraction would add cost and complexity for marginal gains. However, LLM could be valuable for extracting other structured fields (firm names, CNPJ, contract values, scheme descriptions) that the regex does not target.
