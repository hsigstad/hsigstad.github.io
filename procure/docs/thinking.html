Open Questions & Ideas

Thinking and Open Ideas

Current open questions

  • What is the time overlap between court cases and TCE-SP data? TCE-SP licitações cover 2018–2022+. Many court cases involve older procurement (2009–2015). Coverage of older cases will be limited unless we obtain earlier TCE-SP data.
  • Can we use an LLM to extract structured procurement metadata from court case text more reliably than regex? Evaluated: regex captures ~96–100% of true procurement references. LLM adds marginal value for proc numbers but may help for other fields (firm names, CNPJ, values). See docs/linking.md.
  • The BEC data has pre-existing jud_xxx flags on 18,333 OCs (2.4%) — what are these? Who created them? Could be a validation set.
  • The ajustes (contract) files are currently only in the saude project. Resolved: moved to shared /home/henrik/research/data/tce_sp/, symlinked from saude.
  • How many court cases reference procurement processes that fall within the TCE-SP time window?
  • For cases that reference dispensas/contratação sem licitação (no formal process number), is firm-name or CNPJ matching the only option?

Possible directions

Confirmed: court cases are about municipal procurement

The court cases in example_procurement_cases.csv are overwhelmingly about municipal procurement fraud:

  • assunto is almost always "Improbidade Administrativa" or "Dano ao Erário"
  • comarcas span small municipalities across SP (Pontal, Cunha, Igarapava, Tatui, Santos, etc.)
  • cases describe prefeitos directing convites, tomadas de preços, and dispensas at municipal level
  • the procurement numbers (e.g., "convite nº 26/2010 do Município de Pontal") are municipal procedure numbers

The BEC data covers state-level procurement (Secretaria da Educação, etc.) — a different universe.

Confirmed: TCE-SP has bid-level municipal data

The TCE-SP licitação files contain genuine bid-level data:

  • unit of observation: bidder × item × procurement event
  • fields: firm CNPJ, firm name, bid value, outcome (winner/disqualified/no-show)
  • procurement modalities: 97% Pregão Eletrônico, plus Pregão Presencial, Dispensa, Concorrência, Convite, Tomada de Preços, Inexigibilidade
  • scale: ~7.4M rows per month; 500+ municipalities per month
  • coverage: 2018–2022 (saude), Nov–Dec 2024 (procure)

The ajustes (contract) files link to licitação files and add:

  • Numero do processo licitatório + Ano do processo licitatório — the internal municipal process number, which is what court cases cite
  • Codigo da Licitacao — TCE-SP internal code linking to bid-level data
  • contract details: CNPJ, firm name, contract number/dates/values, empenho amounts

Primary linking strategy: court cases → ajustes → licitações

Court case text  →  extract (municipality, modality, processo nº, year)
                           ↓
Ajustes data     →  match on (municipality + processo nº + year) → Codigo da Licitacao
                           ↓
Licitação data   →  join on Codigo da Licitacao → full bid-level detail
  • step 1: extract structured identifiers from court case text (regex or LLM)
  • step 2: join to ajustes on (Municipio + Numero do processo licitatório + Ano do processo licitatório)
  • step 3: use Codigo da Licitacao to get all bids from the licitação files

Complementary strategies

Firm-based linking (no process number needed)

  • extract firm CNPJ from court case text (~3% have explicit CNPJ)
  • extract firm names from court case text (most cases mention firms)
  • match to CNPJ do participante candidato / Nome do participante candidato in licitação data
  • or match to CNPJ da empresa contratada / Nome da empresa contratada in ajustes
  • this finds all procurement activity by firms named in court cases, not just the specific procurement at issue

Municipality-level linking

  • even without matching specific procurements, we can identify which municipalities have corruption cases
  • then study the universe of procurement in those municipalities vs. clean municipalities
  • this is a weaker but higher-coverage strategy

BEC state-level data: secondary role

  • BEC covers state procurement only
  • may be useful for the subset of cases involving state-level purchases
  • the jud_xxx flag (18,333 OCs, 2.4%) could be a validation set
  • keep as supplementary, not primary

What the data exploration revealed

Court case text (3,691 cases)

  • 99.5% mention "licitação" somewhere
  • 70.9% mention "dispensa" (dispensation from competitive bidding)
  • 37.5% mention "convite", 33% "concorrência", 33% "inexigibilidade", 14.6% "pregão", 12.4% "tomada de preços"
  • ~25% have a procurement modality + number in extractable "nº XX/YYYY" format
  • 89% have extractable municipality names
  • 87% have R$ monetary values
  • only 2.6% have CNPJ patterns
  • 26% mention fracionamento, 27% superfaturamento, 24% direcionamento
  • many cases describe irregularities where no formal procurement number exists (contratação sem licitação)

BEC edital files (8 sample files)

  • all are Convite modality, from Secretaria da Educação
  • only identifier is OC number (= filename), no separate "procedimento licitatório" number
  • the edital crosswalk does not work as originally hypothesized (at least for Convite)

BEC procurement data (state-level)

  • consolidated: ~4M rows, 764,538 unique OCs
  • modalities: 49% Convite, 35% Pregão Eletrônico, 15% Dispensa
  • pre-existing jud_xxx flag on 18,333 OCs (2.4%)

TCE-SP municipal licitação data (bid-level)

  • ~7.4M rows per month
  • unit: bidder × item × procurement event
  • outcome field: Classificado - Vencedor (6%), Classificado (11%), Desclassificado (18%), Não Compareceu (61%)
  • 500+ municipalities, 5,000+ licitações per month
  • Numero do edital field present but inconsistently formatted

TCE-SP ajustes data (contract-level)

  • ~4M rows total across 2018–2022
  • has Numero do processo licitatório + Ano do processo licitatório — the key linking field
  • links to licitação via Codigo da Licitacao
  • has contracted firm CNPJ and name, contract values, dates
  • 244,465 unique (municipality, proc_num, proc_year) keys across 644 municipalities
  • processo years in ajustes are concentrated in 2016–2022 (sparse before 2016)

Match test results: court cases → ajustes

See docs/briefs/linking.md for cohesive narrative. Methods in docs/methods.md.

Key finding: the linking chain works well mechanically — match rates of 50–88% within the TCE-SP time window (2017+). The main bottleneck is time period: most court cases reference pre-2016 procurement while ajustes covers 2016–2022.

Methodological sketches

Improving linking match rate

  • obtain older TCE-SP data (highest impact): ajustes pre-2016 would cover ~5,500 currently unmatched references; TCE-SP Portal da Transparência may provide on request
  • CNPJ-based linking: ~650 cases mention firm CNPJs; match to ajustes CNPJ da empresa contratada without needing proc number; works for contratação sem licitação
  • firm-name fuzzy matching: most cases name firms; fuzzy match to Nome da empresa contratada within same municipality
  • expanding court case universe: more recent sentenças (2023–2025) reference procurement in the 2017+ window where match rates are high
  • LLM extraction for other fields: regex handles proc numbers well (~96–100% recall), but LLM could extract firm names, CNPJ, contract values, scheme descriptions

Ideas to explore later

  • network analysis: once linked, can we identify clusters of firms/officials across multiple cases?
  • the jud_xxx flag in BEC data: what is the provenance? could be a ready-made validation set
  • should we also look at CGU audit data (Auditorias/) for a second source of revealed corruption?

Miscellaneous notes

  • not all improbidade cases are procurement-specific — some involve nepotism, misuse of assets, or other non-procurement misconduct
  • about 25-27% of cases describe fracionamento or superfaturamento — these are key corruption scheme types for the paper's typology
  • the TCE-SP SQL dump (in saude) has IBGE municipality codes; the flat CSVs only have municipality names
