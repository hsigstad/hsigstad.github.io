Corruption and discretion

  • Decarolis et al. (2025)

    • topic: rules, discretion, and corruption in Italian government contracting
    • relevance: discretionary auctions linked to corruption only when competition is restricted

  • Szucs (2024)

    • topic: discretion and favoritism in public procurement
    • relevance: buyers manipulate contract values to avoid auctions; discretion increases prices and selects less productive contractors; high discretion benefits politically connected firms

  • Baltrunaite et al. (2021)

    • topic: discretion and supplier selection in public procurement
    • relevance: reform extending discretion increases share of contracts to connected firms, decreases winner productivity

  • Carril (2021)

    • topic: rules versus discretion in public procurement
    • relevance: bunching at thresholds; added scrutiny distorts awards and discourages purchases; more scrutiny worsens ex post performance

  • Hoekman and Taş (2024)

    • topic: discretion and public procurement outcomes in Europe
    • relevance: restrictions on discretion associated with higher average prices

  • Celis Galvez, Titl, and Schotanus (2025)

    • topic: discretion and political favoritism from two reforms
    • relevance: limiting discretion lowers final prices; efficiency gains not seen for politically connected firms

  • Coviello et al. (2022)

    • topic: procurement with manipulation
    • relevance: manipulation below thresholds by appointed officials; reform reduced manipulation and worsened outcomes

  • Coviello, Guglielmo, and Spagnolo (2018)

    • topic: effect of discretion on procurement performance
    • relevance: empirical analysis of discretion effects

  • Fazio (2022)

    • relevance: products purchased with higher discretion 16% more expensive; two-fifths explained by higher quality

Collusion

  • Kawai and Nakabayashi (2022)

    • topic: detecting large-scale collusion in procurement auctions
    • relevance: identification via rebids when all initial bids fail reserve price; over 1,000 firms with non-competitive conduct

  • Conley and Decarolis (2016)

    • topic: detecting bidders groups in collusive auctions
    • relevance: statistical tests for coordinated entry and bidding; validated against court case data

  • Seibel and Škoda (2023)

    • topic: collusion by exclusion in public procurement
    • relevance: bidder preselection allows cartels to exclude rivals; savings lower by 3.7pp in collusive auctions
