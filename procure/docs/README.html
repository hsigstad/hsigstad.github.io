Project Overview

procure

Research question

How is corruption in public procurement organized? Which schemes are used at scale, under what conditions do they arise, and how do firms and officials coordinate to manipulate bidding and contract execution?

Overview

This project provides the first large-scale empirical study of the organizational mechanics of corruption in public procurement. We link the full universe of public contracts and bid histories in Brazil to judicial case files and audit findings, then use large language models to systematically classify corruption schemes and connect them to quantitative procurement patterns.
